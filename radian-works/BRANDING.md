# Brand Direction — Radian Works

## Brand foundation

**Radian Works** is a fictional embedded product studio for ambitious teams
building useful AI products. It joins a client's team for focused stretches of
strategy, product design, and front-end development—turning a promising idea
into a clear product people can understand and adopt.

**Primary tagline:** *Make the next thing obvious.*

**Supporting line:** Radian Works gives early product teams the direction,
design, and build momentum to move from a hard problem to a confident release.

### Purpose

Make emerging technology feel immediately useful to the people it is for.

### Positioning

Radian Works sits between an AI consultancy, product design partner, and
high-craft front-end team. It is a senior, hands-on alternative to a large
agency or a loosely coordinated group of freelancers. The studio does not sell
“AI transformation”; it helps teams decide what to make, make it legible, and
get it into customers' hands.

### Audience

- Founders taking an AI-enabled product from prototype to its first real users.
- Product leaders who need a compact senior team for a pivotal launch.
- Established software teams simplifying a complex AI feature or workflow.
- Technical founders who value sharp product judgment as much as execution.

### Brand pillars

| Pillar | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Useful before impressive | The product has to earn attention by solving a real task. |
| Clarity creates speed | A decisive product story prevents wasted work. |
| Close to the work | Strategy, interface, and implementation stay connected. |
| Built for the handoff | Every engagement leaves a team with a usable system, not a black box. |

## Personality and tone

Radian sounds like the sharpest, most grounded person in a product room:
curious, direct, calm under ambiguity, and generous with expertise. It is
confident without hype, technical without obscuring the point, and energetic
without performing urgency.

| Trait | Use | Avoid |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Clear | Short sentences and concrete outcomes | Buzzwords and inflated claims |
| Energetic | Active verbs and decisive invitations | Pressure language or false scarcity |
| Practical | Explain what happens next | Abstract “innovation” talk |
| Human | Speak to teams and customers as people | Cold, platform-first wording |

### Voice rules

- Lead with the customer or product outcome, not the technology.
- Name hard decisions plainly: choose, simplify, test, launch, learn.
- Let confidence come from specificity rather than superlatives.
- Keep navigation and labels extremely short.
- Use “we” for the studio and “your team” for the client; avoid calling the
  audience “users” when a more specific role is known.

## Copy direction

### Core message

Radian helps product teams turn technical possibility into an experience that
people immediately know how to use.

### Message hierarchy

1. **What we make:** AI products that are clear, useful, and ready to ship.
2. **How we work:** A compact embedded team across direction, design, and build.
3. **Why it matters:** Better product choices create faster, more confident launches.
4. **Invitation:** Bring the question your team has not solved yet.

### Sample homepage copy

- **Hero eyebrow:** Embedded AI product studio
- **Hero headline:** Make the next thing obvious.
- **Hero supporting copy:** We help ambitious teams shape, design, and ship AI
  products people can use from day one.
- **Primary CTA:** Start a project
- **Secondary CTA:** See the work
- **Capabilities heading:** The right momentum for the pivotal parts.
- **Closing heading:** Have a hard product question?
- **Closing CTA:** Talk with Radian

### Naming conventions

- Services: Product Direction, Experience Design, Launch Systems.
- Case studies should use fictional company names plus a clear outcome, such as
  “Pace — Making financial planning feel actionable.”
- Buttons begin with verbs: Start, See, Meet, Read, Build.
- Microcopy is concise and reassuring: “Replies within two working days.”

## Visual direction

### Creative idea

**A clear signal through a shifting field.** The site pairs decisive editorial
type with soft, atmospheric light fields. Structure stays stable and readable
while coloured energy moves at the edges—an expression of Radian's role in
making a complex product feel focused.

The main reference informs the experience at a high level: a generous rounded
hero, compact floating navigation, a large central promise, soft depth, and a
small interactive visual object. Radian changes the identity, palette,
typography, graphic language, imagery, copy, and layout proportions so the
result is distinctly its own.

### Keywords

Decisive, luminous, tactile, intelligent, spacious, precise, quietly kinetic.

### Colour system

| Role | Name | Hex | Use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Primary ink | Night Signal | `#111827` | Type, dark UI, footer |
| Canvas | Mineral | `#F3F2EE` | Overall page background |
| Light surface | Halo | `#FCFCFA` | Cards, navigation, inputs |
| Primary signal | Electric Lime | `#C7F55A` | CTA emphasis, active moments |
| Secondary signal | Iris | `#8E8CFF` | Data, highlights, visual accents |
| Warm counterpoint | Solar | `#FFB470` | Small editorial accents only |
| Hero pulse | Ember | `#FF7043` | Hero signal capsule and its ambient glow |
| Muted text | Graphite | `#5D6470` | Supporting copy and metadata |

Night Signal and Mineral establish calm contrast. Electric Lime is the primary
interactive signal; Ember is reserved for the hero's focused signal capsule.
Iris and Solar appear sparingly inside art and data-like details. Avoid
full-page gradients or using every accent in one component.

### Typography

- **Display:** `Manrope` (600–800). Its clean, geometric proportions make the
  product story feel current, precise, and immediately legible at large scale.
- **Body/UI:** `Manrope` (400–700). A single-family system keeps labels,
  metadata, and long copy disciplined while the larger display weights create
  hierarchy.
- **Optional editorial detail:** `IBM Plex Mono` (500) for small timestamps,
  phases, and measurement-style labels only.

| Level | Font | Desktop | Mobile |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| H1 | Manrope 600 | 76–108px | 48–64px |
| H2 | Manrope 600 | 48–64px | 36–44px |
| H3 | Manrope 700 | 22–28px | 20–24px |
| Body | Manrope 400 | 16–18px | 16px |
| Label | Manrope 700 | 11–13px | 11–12px |

### Layout and interface

- Use a wide, rounded hero frame set inside a Mineral page canvas; its corners
  should feel substantial, not card-like (28–36px desktop).
- Navigation is a slim, floating Halo capsule with soft translucency and a
  restrained shadow. It should never feel like a separate app shell.
- Keep the primary headline centered or optically centered; surrounding details
  stay quiet and leave it room to land.
- Mix a stable 12-column content grid with occasional offset case-study images
  and small “signal” annotations.
- Buttons are compact rounded rectangles, not oversized pills. Dark primary
  actions get a crisp Lime hover glow; secondary actions stay light and outlined.
- Use one visually expressive object in the hero (a floating modular form or
  product tokens), then make later sections more editorial and content-led.

### Graphic language

- Abstract “signal objects” use translucent resin, frosted acrylic, and soft
  refraction rather than generic AI brains, chat bubbles, or circuit imagery.
- Small square and capsule tokens can represent product inputs, decisions, and
  outcomes. Keep them few and deliberately placed.
- Use a fine radial line, coordinate marks, or small mono labels as secondary
  details—not as a dominant sci-fi grid.
- Shadows are broad, cool, and low-opacity. Borders are thin and warm-gray.

## Image direction

Photography and generated visuals should make technical work feel tangible and
human. Use images as atmosphere and proof, not decoration.

### Hero art

Create an original 3D composition of three to five modular translucent objects
in Night Signal, Electric Lime, and Iris. Place it against a soft mineral field
with a broad lime-to-lilac light bloom at one edge. The objects should suggest
ideas connecting and becoming useful—not floating app logos or copied UI tiles.

**Prompt framework:** “Abstract modular product tokens made from frosted resin
and translucent acrylic, midnight navy with electric lime and soft iris accents,
on a warm mineral-gray studio background, large diffused side light, subtle
refraction, premium product photography, macro 50mm lens, generous negative
space, tactile and believable, no text, no logos, no interface screenshots.”

### Case-study art

- Pair product-interface crops with real process imagery: hands annotating a
  prototype, material swatches, workshop tables, printed maps, or architectural
  light.
- Give each fictional project one signature material or visual metaphor.
- Preserve a restrained palette per story; do not reuse the hero’s entire colour
  system in every card.
- Use natural, side-lit photography with gentle grain. Prefer documentary
  crops to staged team photos.

### Accessibility

- Provide meaningful alt text for every informative image and describe the
  product outcome, not only its colours.
- Do not place essential copy over the brightest area of an image.
- Decorative signal objects receive empty alt text and are hidden from screen
  readers.

## Motion direction

Motion conveys focus arriving, not constant activity.

- Headline lines fade and rise by 16–24px with a 450–600ms ease-out entrance.
- Hero signal objects drift independently by 8–18px and gently rotate; no
  looping movement should compete with reading.
- Navigation, buttons, and cards respond within 180–260ms using
  `cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1)`.
- Case-study images can scale to `1.02–1.03` on hover; labels should move less
  than the image.
- Scroll reveals happen once per section. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` by
  showing all content without parallax or continuous movement.

## Initial website structure

1. Hero: core promise, primary invitation, understated signal object.
2. Capabilities: the three engagement areas and their concrete outputs.
3. Selected work: three case studies that demonstrate clarity, not visual variety alone.
4. Working model: a short, transparent sequence from question to handoff.
5. Proof: selected metrics, client observations, or principles.
6. FAQ: scope, pace, collaboration, and technical handoff.
7. Closing contact invitation and compact footer.

## One-line implementation brief

Build Radian Works as a luminous, high-clarity AI product studio: a generous,
calm editorial site where a focused visual signal and direct copy make complex
work feel ready to move.
