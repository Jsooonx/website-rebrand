# Full Brand Direction — Cinder Atelier

## Brand foundation

**Cinder Atelier** is a fictional independent creative practice for brands that need a stronger presence in the world. It combines art direction, visual identity, campaign systems, and digital experiences for culture, fashion, hospitality, music, and new luxury.

The name holds two tensions: **cinder** is the ember left after a bright moment; **atelier** is the place where that moment is made with care. The identity turns that tension into a point of view: dark foundations, precise gestures of light, and work that leaves a trace.

**Primary tagline:** *Leave a mark in the dark.*

**Supporting line:** Cinder Atelier shapes visual worlds for ambitious brands before the world learns how to name them.

### Purpose

Make a brand's point of view visible, felt, and hard to forget.

### Positioning

Cinder sits between a creative direction studio, an identity partner, and a digital image-maker. It is not a high-volume content agency or a template-led web shop. Clients work directly with a compact senior team that can establish a visual idea, turn it into a system, and make it move across image, campaign, and screen.

### Audience

- Fashion, beauty, hospitality, and lifestyle founders with a distinctive product but an indistinct visual world.
- Cultural institutions, music labels, and independent publishers preparing a pivotal launch.
- Premium digital brands that need their website to feel like an experience, not a catalogue.
- Marketing and creative leaders seeking a focused, hands-on partner for a visible reintroduction.

## Personality and voice

Cinder sounds like an exacting creative director with warmth beneath the surface: observant, composed, culturally aware, and clear about the decision at hand. It writes in short, confident lines. Mystery is welcome; vagueness is not.

| Trait | Do | Avoid |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Cinematic | Use sensory but concrete language | Purple prose or empty drama |
| Assured | State a point of view plainly | Inflated claims and trend chasing |
| Selective | Let a few words carry weight | Dense blocks of marketing copy |
| Human | Speak directly to collaborators | Detached luxury clichés |

### Voice rules

- Lead with what people will see, feel, or recognise.
- Prefer active verbs: frame, reveal, direct, sharpen, carry, compose.
- Use “we” for the practice and “your brand” or the specific audience for the client.
- Keep navigation and CTA labels short: Work, Practice, Contact, Begin.
- Avoid “disruptive,” “elevated,” “iconic,” and generic AI language.

## Visual direction

### Creative idea: a line of heat

The defining visual gesture is a single **line of heat**: an incandescent, controlled stroke that crosses a dark, tactile visual field. It can frame a face, trace an architectural edge, follow a hand, or guide the eye through a layout. It suggests instinct meeting precision—an idea becoming visible.

The reference establishes a high-level atmosphere only: a near-black canvas, editorial portraiture, oversized typography, and a warm light gesture. Cinder must use entirely original people, photography, art direction, copy, and layout proportions. Do not recreate a face, wardrobe, glowing path, or composition from a reference.

### Keywords

Nocturnal, incandescent, sculptural, intimate, precise, physical, editorial.

### Colour system

| Role | Name | Hex | Use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Primary ground | Coal | `#111415` | Page fields, hero, footer |
| Lifted dark | Char | `#1B2021` | Cards, panels, image overlays |
| Primary paper | Bone | `#F1EEE8` | Display type and light surfaces |
| Signal | Cinder Orange | `#FF5A1F` | Heat line, primary emphasis, small active states |
| Warm bloom | Furnace | `#FF9A52` | Ambient glow and sparing falloff |
| Cool counterpoint | Oxide Blue | `#7CA0A4` | Secondary data, fine detail, image treatment |
| Quiet text | Ash | `#A6ADAC` | Metadata and supporting copy |

Coal and Bone do most of the work. Cinder Orange is memorable precisely because it is limited: use it to guide, signal, or punctuate—not to fill every button or section. Any glow must feel like emitted light, never a generic gradient.

### Typography

- **Display:** `Archivo Black` or `Neue Montreal` Black (fallback: `Arial Black`). The display face should be condensed through tracking and scale, blunt and editorial rather than decorative.
- **Body/UI:** `Inter` or `Helvetica Neue` (400–600). Keep interface language clean, compact, and readable.
- **Technical detail:** `IBM Plex Mono` (500) for locations, timestamps, project codes, and captions only.

| Level | Font | Desktop | Mobile |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| H1 | Display Black | 112–180px | 60–88px |
| H2 | Display Black | 64–96px | 42–56px |
| H3 | Inter SemiBold | 20–26px | 18–22px |
| Body | Inter Regular | 16–18px | 16px |
| Label | Inter SemiBold / Mono | 11–12px | 11px |

Use tight display tracking (`-0.06em` to `-0.09em`) and compact leading (`0.78–0.9`) only at large sizes. Body copy remains open and calm.

### Layout and interface

- Begin with a near-viewport hero: visual image at the centre, utility details at the outer edges, and a two-line statement anchoring the lower third.
- Use a disciplined 12-column grid. Let the hero break the grid once through type or image crop; later sections restore structure.
- Navigation is spare: wordmark at left, contact at centre or right, compact menu trigger at the opposite edge. No large persistent app bar.
- Use thin `rgba(241,238,232,.14)` rules and small mono labels to establish editorial rhythm.
- Surfaces are flat and dark. Use translucent overlays only when they improve legibility over photography.
- Buttons are low-radius or square-cornered. Primary states may receive a thin orange edge or contained heat bloom; avoid oversized pills.

### Graphic language

- The line of heat may be a real photographed light trace, a carefully rendered filament, or a motion path. It must follow the scene's geometry.
- Pair glossy light with tangible surfaces: leather, metal, glass, skin, stone, paper, and fabric.
- Crop images closely and asymmetrically. Give one image per section authority instead of using a collage everywhere.
- Use project identifiers such as `01 / OBLIQUE`, locations, dates, and short coordinates as quiet framing devices.
- Never use generic neon blobs, wireframe grids, floating app screenshots, or faux film grain as a substitute for a concept.

## Image direction

Photography should be original, tactile, and physically plausible. The world is lit as though a warm practical source has moved through a dark set, with a cool fill retaining detail in the shadows.

### Hero art

Create an original editorial image that uses its own subject and composition: a sculptural object, a performer in textured fabric, a close architectural surface, or a distinct still life. A single warm filament or reflected strip of light can move through the scene, but it must not repeat the reference pose, face, garment, or path.

**Prompt framework:** “Original editorial art direction for an independent creative studio, [unique subject], charcoal-black set, textured [material], one controlled incandescent orange practical light tracing a compositionally distinct path, subtle cool cyan fill in deep shadow, premium fashion campaign photography, medium-format 80mm lens, rich material detail, cinematic contrast, negative space for typography, no text, no logo, no copied pose or composition.”

### Accessibility

- Every informative image needs alt text that explains its content and relation to the project; do not describe only colours or aesthetics.
- The heat line is decorative unless it conveys state or direction. Decorative instances use empty alt text and are hidden from screen readers.
- Preserve a readable dark scrim wherever display text crosses photography.
- Information and interactive state cannot rely on orange alone.

## Motion direction

Motion should feel like a camera finding focus and an ember travelling through material—not like a visual effect demo.

- Hero type enters in two deliberate blocks: opacity `0→1`, `y: 28px→0`, over 500–650ms with `cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1)`.
- The heat line may draw once on entry or gently breathe at 3–5% opacity. Avoid continuous rapid movement.
- Images shift by no more than 4–8% within clipped frames during scroll; use parallax only where it reinforces depth.
- Menu and accordion transitions should complete in 180–260ms.
- Project cards may raise image scale to `1.02` and bring a project code into focus on hover.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`: show content in its final state and disable line drawing, parallax, marquee, and auto-looping animation.

## Website structure

1. **Hero / Manifesto** — Full-screen identity, current location/time detail, statement, selected-project count, and a visual line of heat.
2. **Selected visions** — Four large project stories with a short outcome, discipline, and year.
3. **The practice** — Why Cinder works from image outward: observation, direction, system, release.
4. **Capabilities** — Art Direction, Visual Identity, Digital Experience, and Launch Worlds, presented as an expandable, concise system.
5. **Signal study** — A pinned editorial sequence showing an idea evolving from material reference to campaign and digital expression.
6. **Recognition / voices** — A restrained row of fictional collaborator notes and selected features; keep proof specific rather than logo-heavy.
7. **FAQ** — Scope, timing, remote collaboration, and launch handoff.
8. **Contact** — Large invitation, email, location, availability, and compact social links.
